Welcome to FXDGear

Fixed gear bikes, often nicknamed "fixies," stand out for their simplicity, direct connection to the ride, and undeniable urban style. Unlike traditional bikes, fixies have a single gear and no freewheel mechanism. This means the pedals and rear wheel are locked together, creating a unique feel where the rider is constantly engaged with the bike's movement. This simplicity translates to both low maintenance needs and a lightweight design, making fixies ideal for navigating city streets. They also offer a chance to develop skills like balance and coordination as mastering a fixie requires a different kind of control compared to multi-geared bikes.

However, it's important to be aware of some limitations. Fixies don't allow coasting, meaning the pedals spin whenever the wheels are rolling. Additionally, some lack brakes, which can be illegal or dangerous depending on your location. Finally, their single gear makes climbing hills a more challenging endeavor. Overall, fixed gear bikes cater to a specific niche of riders who value simplicity, style, and a direct connection to their ride. While they may not be ideal for everyone, they offer a distinct and engaging cycling experience, especially for those navigating flat, urban environments.
